Mobile Robotic Arm

A mobile manipulator platform combining a wheeled base with a robotic arm for flexible object handling, research demos, and modern automation tasks.

ROS C++ CAD Kinematics Embedded Systems

Problem Statement

Industrial manipulators are powerful but usually fixed in place, while mobile robots can navigate but often cannot perform dexterous interaction. This project explored a scalable mobile robotic arm that could move through an environment and perform manipulation tasks from different positions.

Approach

The system was designed as a modular robotics platform. The base handled mobility and power distribution, while the arm handled manipulation. Mechanical design, electronics, control logic, and motion planning were developed together so the robot could remain stable while reaching and grasping.

Solution

  • Designed a mobile base and arm structure with an emphasis on serviceability and modularity.
  • Implemented motor control for locomotion and joint actuation.
  • Worked through kinematic constraints for reach, payload, and base stability.
  • Documented the system as a scalable platform for modern robotics applications.
This project became an IEEE PuneCon 2023 publication: "Revolutionizing Robotics: A Scalable and Versatile Mobile Robotic Arm for Modern Applications."

Challenges

The most important tradeoff was stability versus reach. A longer arm improves workspace, but it also shifts the center of mass and increases torque demand. The design required iteration across frame geometry, actuator selection, and safe operating limits.

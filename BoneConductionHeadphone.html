Bone Conduction Headphone

A wearable audio prototype that transmits sound through vibration while keeping the ear canal open for environmental awareness.

Product Design CAD Electronics 3D Printing Wearables

Problem Statement

Conventional headphones block the ear canal, which can be unsafe for users who need to remain aware of their surroundings. The goal was to prototype a compact bone conduction headphone that could deliver usable audio while remaining comfortable and mechanically stable.

Approach

The design balanced audio transmission, contact pressure, wearable comfort, and manufacturability. The enclosure needed to position the transducers firmly against the cheekbone area without creating excessive pressure during longer use.

Solution

  • Designed a lightweight frame and transducer housing in CAD.
  • Integrated bone conduction transducers with a compact amplifier circuit.
  • Used 3D printed iterations to test fit, contact angle, and durability.
  • Refined the geometry to improve comfort and keep vibration transfer consistent.
The project was a hardware learning exercise in how small mechanical changes can make a large difference in perceived audio quality.

Challenges

Bone conduction performance depends heavily on contact placement and pressure. Too little pressure weakens the audio, while too much makes the device uncomfortable. The prototype required multiple fit iterations to find a workable balance.

Jewellery Background Removal

A segmentation pipeline for extracting jewellery from noisy product photos while preserving fine edges, reflections, stones, and metallic details.

PyTorch OpenCV ONNX Mask Refinement Product Imaging

Problem Statement

Jewellery catalogs need clean product images, but manual background removal is slow and expensive. Generic segmentation tools often fail on rings, chains, thin edges, transparent stones, and glossy reflections because the boundary between product and background can be visually subtle.

Approach

I focused the pipeline on jewellery-specific failure cases rather than treating the problem as ordinary foreground extraction. The model predicted a coarse foreground mask, then classical image processing refined the border and removed isolated artifacts.

Validation emphasized visual quality at the edges: prongs, circular ring interiors, gemstones, shadows, and reflective metal surfaces all needed to survive the extraction process.

Solution

  • Prepared labelled jewellery image pairs for model training and qualitative validation.
  • Used a custom segmentation model to predict foreground masks for rings and other jewellery assets.
  • Applied mask cleanup, contour filtering, and alpha edge smoothing for production-ready cutouts.
  • Exported the model for lower-latency inference and easier product integration.
The project helped reduce dependency on third-party background-removal APIs by moving the core segmentation step in-house.

Challenges

The hardest cases were high-shine products on light backgrounds, transparent stones, and tiny holes inside rings. Over-aggressive cleanup made the jewellery look fake, while under-cleanup left visible background noise. The final system balanced model output with conservative post-processing.
